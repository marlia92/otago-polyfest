# The Story Behind *Te Moananui ā Kiwa*: The Banners That Tell Our Story

If you've attended Otago Polyfest over the past decade, you've almost certainly stood beneath our iconic Te Moananui ā Kiwa banners.

Towering above the stage, these vibrant artworks have become synonymous with Otago Polyfest. They frame thousands of performances each year and provide a powerful reminder that every group who steps onto our stage is part of a much larger story.

What many people don't know is how these taonga came to be.

## A Community Collaboration

In 2012, after nearly twenty years of service, the original Polyfest stage banners had reached the end of their life. Rather than simply replacing them, the Otago Polyfest Committee saw an opportunity to create something that truly reflected the cultures, values and people who make our festival unique.

Working alongside Otago Polytechnic's School of Art and Design, artists Heramāhina Eketone and Tere Moeroa developed the artistic vision, while Communication Design staff and students—including Caroline McCaw, Thomas Te Whaiti-Henry, Madison Henry-Ryan and Vincent Egan—transformed the concepts into the large-scale digital artworks that now stand proudly behind our stage.

The project became far more than a design exercise. It was a true example of collaboration, bringing together artists, designers, educators and the Polyfest committee to create something that would serve the community for generations to come.

## The Meaning of *Te Moananui ā Kiwa*

*Te Moananui ā Kiwa* speaks to the great Pacific Ocean that connects Aotearoa with our Pacific neighbours.

The artwork celebrates the ancestral journeys of our tūpuna, honours Māori as tangata whenua, acknowledges the many Pacific nations represented at Otago Polyfest, and reflects the relationships that continue to unite our communities today.

Throughout the design are symbols representing knowledge, whakapapa, leadership, resilience, creativity and growth. Traditional Māori motifs sit alongside patterns inspired by Pacific cultures, weaving together many identities into one shared visual story.

Just as every performance at Polyfest is unique, every element within the banners carries meaning, reminding us that while our cultures are distinct, we are connected through shared histories, shared oceans and shared futures.

## Learning Through Collaboration

The creation of the banners also became an invaluable learning experience for the Otago Polytechnic design students involved.

Working alongside experienced artists, they contributed hundreds of hours developing the intricate digital artwork while gaining real-world experience in collaborative design. Several students went on to secure professional opportunities following the project's success.

The project embodied many of the values Otago Polyfest continues to champion today: partnership, mentorship, creativity and learning through doing.

## A Living Symbol of Otago Polyfest

More than a decade later, the *Te Moananui ā Kiwa* banners remain one of the most recognisable symbols of Otago Polyfest.

For thousands of performers, they have become the backdrop to first performances, lifelong memories, proud cultural expression and moments shared with whānau and community.

Every year, as the banners are raised once again, they remind us that Otago Polyfest is more than a festival. It is a place where cultures are celebrated, young people are empowered, and communities come together.
