# Taking the Leap for Otago Polyfest

Sometimes supporting Otago Polyfest means months of planning, countless meetings and late nights behind a computer.

Sometimes... it means stepping backwards off the roof of Forsyth Barr Stadium.

This year, Otago Polyfest was incredibly grateful to be chosen as the beneficiary of Toss the Boss Ōtepoti 2026, a community fundraising event that challenged local leaders to face their fears by abseiling 35 metres from the stadium roof—all in support of a cause they believe in.

The event brought together business leaders, educators, community organisations and passionate supporters from across Ōtepoti, each fundraising to help ensure Otago Polyfest continues to thrive for future generations.

Every donation, every share and every encouraging message helped make a difference.

### More Than a Fundraiser

Otago Polyfest is one of the South Island's largest celebrations of Māori and Pacific language, culture and identity.

Each year, thousands of tamariki and rangatahi take to our stages with pride, supported by their schools, whānau, tutors and communities. Behind every performance is an enormous amount of planning, infrastructure and volunteer effort that often goes unseen. Funds raised through initiatives like Toss the Boss help support the many essential costs involved in delivering the festival—from staging and technical production to venue costs, traffic management and the countless behind-the-scenes details that make the event possible.

### A Community Effort

One of the things we love most about Toss the Boss is that it reflects something we often say at Otago Polyfest:

**It takes a village.**

Seeing so many people step outside their comfort zone to champion our rangatahi was both humbling and inspiring. Whether you donated, shared the campaign, nominated a participant or simply cheered from the sidelines, you played a part in supporting the future of Otago Polyfest.

To everyone who participated, donated and supported this year's campaign—**thank you**.

Your generosity helps ensure that thousands of young people can continue to celebrate their language, culture and identity through Otago Polyfest for years to come.

Ngā mihi nui.
